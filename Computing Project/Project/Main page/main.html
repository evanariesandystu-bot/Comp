Student Regulation: Mobile Phone & Device Policy

This regulation explains acceptable use, consequences for misuse, and mentoring procedures related to mobile phones and electronic devices during school hours. The goal is to support learning, ensure safety, and maintain classroom focus.

Key Guidelines

  • Students may bring mobile phones to school but must keep them turned off during lessons.
  • Phones must be submitted to the homeroom teacher during morning devotion.
  • Phones may be used in class only with the subject teacher's permission to support learning.
  • Confiscated phones will be returned to parents after the specified period.
  • Repeated misuse results in progressively stricter consequences and mentoring.

Consequences (Progression)

  1. Verbal warning and recorded red offense slip.
  2. Confiscation for 3 days; community service assigned and parental notification.
  3. If repeated: confiscation until report-card day, formal warning letters, and referral to Student Affairs.

Mentoring & Support

Students who misuse devices will receive mentoring that includes regular reporting to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs, counseling sessions with the school counselor, and guidance from the homeroom teacher to help correct behavior and reintegrate positively into class.
